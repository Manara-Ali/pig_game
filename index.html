Game Rules

Players take turns rolling the die. Each time, a player repeatedly rolls a die until either a 1 is rolled or the player decides to "hold" their accumulated rolling score.

In the event where a player rolls a 1, they score nothing, lose their turn and it becomes the next player's turn to roll the dice. In the player rolls any other number, that roll of dice is added to their previous roll, if any, and the accumulated roll total for that player get displayed and the player's turn continues. In the event where a player chooses to "hold", meaning to "cash out" their accumulated roll total, that total is added to their current score, and it becomes the next player's turn.

For example, the first player, Donald, begins a turn with a roll of 5. Donald could hold and score 5 points, but chooses to roll again. Donald rolls a 2, and could hold with a turn total of 7 points, but chooses to roll again. Donald rolls a 1, and must end his turn without scoring. The next player, Alexis, rolls the sequence 4-5-3-5-5, after which she chooses to hold, and adds her turn total of 22 points to her score.
